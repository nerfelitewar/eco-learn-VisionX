Challenges for Class 1-8

These real-life challenges are thoughtfully designed for younger students, helping them build essential life skills, develop responsibility, and cultivate problem-solving habits at an early age. They are engaging yet achievable, encouraging creativity and confidence in everyday situations.

Back to Home

Explore the Challenges

Choose a challenge and start making a positive impact on the environment!

Plastic-Free Day

Avoid single-use plastics for one day.

Save Water

Track and reduce daily water usage.

Tree Spotting

Identify 5 local trees and learn their benefits.

Recycling Hunt

Find three reusable or recyclable items at home.

Energy Saver

Switch off unused devices to save electricity.

Turn Off Lights

Turn off lights when leaving a room and unplug chargers not in use.

Plant and Nurture

Plant and nurture a small garden or tree.

Adopt a Spot

Adopt a section of a yard or park and keep it clean.

Timed Showers

Time showers with a fun song to save water.

Recycling Chief

Be the "Recycling Chief" to sort waste properly.

Nature Sketch

Draw your favorite tree, flower, or bird in your neighborhood.

Upcycle Craft

Make a pencil holder or toy from waste material.

Eco Diary

Write one eco-friendly action you did each day for a week.

Lunch Box Check

Bring a zero-waste lunch (no plastic wraps or disposable cutlery).

Walk or Cycle Day

Walk or cycle instead of taking a car for a short trip.

Paper Saver

Use both sides of paper for notes or art.

Seed Collection

Collect and plant seeds from fruits you eat at home.

Water Watcher

Place a bucket under dripping taps and measure wasted water.

Eco Quiz

Make a small quiz for friends or family on saving the environment.

Bird Feeder

Make a simple bird feeder and record the birds visiting.
