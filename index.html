Metric Value Notes / Explanation
CO₂ Emissions 3.44 billion tonnes CO₂e per year India is the 3rd largest emitter globally, with energy, transport, and industry being the main contributors.
Water Use 700 billion cubic meters per year Agriculture accounts for ~85% of India's freshwater use, mainly for rice, wheat, sugarcane, and cotton.
Forest Cover 71.7 million hectares (~21.7% of land area) Includes dense and open forests as per FSI (Forest Survey of India) 2021 report.
Annual Forest Loss 132,000 hectares per year Caused by agriculture expansion, mining, shifting cultivation, and urbanization.
Net Gain in Tree Cover Positive Despite localized forest loss, massive afforestation drives (e.g., CAMPA, state-level plantation programs) add tree cover yearly.
CO₂ Sequestration 331 million tonnes CO₂ absorbed per year Forests and tree cover offset ~9–10% of India's annual emissions, acting as a major carbon sink.

About EcoLearn

EcoLearn is dedicated to transforming environmental education through innovative gamification and real-world impact. Our mission is to empower students worldwide to become proactive environmental stewards by making learning engaging, interactive, and impactful.

Our Mission

We align with India's National Education Policy (NEP) 2020 and the United Nations Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs). Our platform fosters environmental awareness and action through gamified challenges, interactive quizzes, and real-world impact tracking.

By engaging students in meaningful activities, we aim to cultivate a generation of eco-conscious leaders who will drive sustainable change globally.
